WaterTag is a perfect opportunity for event businesses.

WaterTag is more than a fun water game. It is a mobile, structured, business-ready attraction that can help camps, resorts, schools, team-building providers, and event companies create a new revenue stream without building a permanent venue.

Explore Business Model Read The Post
Why it works

The events industry needs attractions that are fresh, mobile, and easy to sell.

Many event companies already offer inflatables, field games, foam parties, or basic water activities. The challenge is finding something that feels new, but is still simple for customers to understand. WaterTag fits that space perfectly.

🚚

Mobile setup

WaterTag can be brought to the customer instead of depending on a fixed location. That makes it attractive for operators that work across different venues.

👥

Group-friendly activity

The game is designed for teams, rotations, missions, and group sessions, which makes it useful for camps, resorts, schools, and corporate events.

📸

Built for social content

Water, movement, teams, and competition naturally create photos and videos that help operators promote the next event.

Business logic

WaterTag is not just equipment. It is a sellable experience.

The strongest business opportunities are repeatable. WaterTag gives operators a clear product they can package, price, staff, market, and sell to many types of clients.

  • Easy to explain to customers in one sentence.
  • Suitable for outdoor events and warm-weather markets.
  • Works as a standalone attraction or add-on to existing packages.
  • Can be sold to recurring clients like camps, schools, resorts, and companies.
  • Creates a premium alternative to casual water games.
1

Package the activity

Sell WaterTag as a full event package, not just equipment. Include game rounds, rules, setup, staff, and a clear session format.

2

Target the right buyers

Focus on buyers that already need group activities: camps, hotels, resorts, schools, youth groups, team-building companies, and family events.

3

Create repeat demand

A camp can book it every summer. A resort can add it to guest entertainment. A company can use it for team-building. That repeat potential is the real opportunity.

4

Use content to sell faster

Every session creates strong visual content. Photos and videos make the attraction easier to market because people immediately understand the experience.

Bottom line: WaterTag is a business opportunity because it is easy to understand, easy to market, and easy to repeat.
